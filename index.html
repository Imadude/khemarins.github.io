About

I am a first-year undergraduate at the University of Melbourne, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and Statistics. My interests lie broadly in applied mathematics and statistics — from mathematical modelling and optimisation to stochastic processes and data-driven inference.

I am currently working with Prof. Lin on a research article concerning constrained quadratic programming, exploring connections between convex function theory, level-set geometry, and analytical solution methods.

Before Melbourne, I completed the International Baccalaureate at Li Po Chun United World College in Hong Kong, graduating as Dux with a score of 44/45.

Epidemiological Modelling

Hong Kong, 2024

Constructed and analysed a vaccination-augmented epidemiological model using coupled differential equations and eigenvalue-based stability analysis. Estimated transmission dynamics and herd-immunity thresholds (~47%).

Independent Research

Queueing Theory & Hospital Systems

Hong Kong, 2024

Applied stochastic processes and queueing theory (Markov chains, M/M/c models) to hospital systems, deriving steady-state distributions and expected waiting times. Validated results via simulation.
